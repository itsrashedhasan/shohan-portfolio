B.Sc. Thesis · Target: IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging

Privacy-Preserving Federated Domain Generalization for Multi-Centre Cardiac Cine MRI Segmentation (F²DG-nnU-Net)

Novel federated learning framework built on nnU-Net v2 for LV/RV/MYO segmentation on the M&Ms cardiac MRI dataset (4 vendors, 6 centres). Combines client-level Fourier-amplitude fingerprint sharing (privacy-preserving), amplitude-mixing for synthetic domain augmentation, and boundary-weighted Jensen–Shannon consistency loss. Evaluated with leave-one-vendor-out and data-scarcity ablations.

In Preparation Federated Learning Medical Imaging PyTorch First Author

Active Research · Target: IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging

Federated learning framework combining sparse regularization (reduces communication overhead, prevents client drift), co-training across federated clients, and domain adaptation for heterogeneous hospital imaging equipment. Addresses real-world non-IID data, domain shift, and privacy constraints (HIPAA/GDPR) in multi-institutional medical AI.

About Rashedul Hasan Shohan

I'm Rashedul Hasan Shohan, born on July 21, 2003, in Khalishpur, Khulna, Bangladesh. From a very early age, I was drawn to understanding how things work — not just accepting them as they are. That curiosity grew into a deep drive to learn, create, and explore, shaping me into someone who sees every challenge as an opportunity to build something meaningful.

My academic foundation was built at Rotary School Khulna, where I completed my SSC with a perfect GPA of 5.00, and at Govt. Hazi Mohammad Mohsin College, where I secured a GPA of 5.00 in HSC under the Science group. Beyond the classroom, I actively participated in social and community organizations — experiences that taught me leadership, responsibility, and the value of working with people toward a shared purpose.

Before entering university, I built a strong creative foundation through 3+ years of freelancing on Upwork as a Graphic and UI/UX Designer. Working with international clients across branding, interface design, and visual storytelling taught me not just design craft but also professionalism, client communication, and delivering quality under real-world pressure. Outside of work, cricket and football remain my reset — sports that reinforce the same values I bring to everything I do: teamwork, discipline, and resilience.

Since 2022, I've been pursuing a B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering at Daffodil International University, maintaining a CGPA of 3.91 out of 4.00. My academic journey spans full-stack web development, mobile application design, machine learning, deep learning, federated learning, data science, and AI research. I've built 16+ projects across these domains — from Laravel marketplaces and Flutter mobile apps to IoT systems and OpenGL simulations — each one pushing me to grow further as an engineer.

On the research front, I serve as a Research Assistant at the Health and Informatics Research Lab, DIU, where I work on medical AI, federated learning, and explainable systems. I am the first author of two accepted international conference papers — one published in Springer LNNS (SCOPUS-indexed) presented at Lasell University, USA, and another accepted at IEEE/IFAC ICCAD 2026 in Lisbon, Portugal, with international collaboration from Prince Sultan University and Cairo University. My B.Sc. thesis — a privacy-preserving federated learning framework for cardiac MRI segmentation — is actively targeting IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, one of the most prestigious journals in the field.

Alongside research, I have worked as a Python Instructor, designing and delivering structured training in data science and machine learning — guiding learners through NumPy, Pandas, Scikit-learn, and applied model development with a project-based, outcome-focused approach. I also served as General Secretary of the Health and Informatics Research Lab, coordinating lab operations, research workshops, and collaboration between members and faculty.

Today, I stand at the intersection of research, engineering, and design — driven by one belief: that technology, built thoughtfully, can solve real human problems. Whether it's building a privacy-preserving AI for hospitals, a marketplace for Bangladeshi students, or a multimodal medical chatbot — every project I take on carries that same intent. My story is still unfolding, my best work is still ahead, and this is only the beginning.
